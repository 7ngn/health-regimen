Proper Form and Technique for All Workout Plan Exercises

UPPER BODY EXERCISES

CHEST EXERCISES

Barbell Bench Press

Setup:

  • Lie on bench with eyes under the bar
  • Grip bar slightly wider than shoulder-width
  • Plant feet firmly on floor
  • Maintain natural arch in lower back

Execution:

  1. Unrack bar and position over chest
  2. Lower bar to chest in controlled motion (2-3 seconds)
  3. Touch chest lightly at nipple line
  4. Press bar up explosively, keeping elbows at 45-degree angle
  5. Lock out arms at top

Common Mistakes: Bouncing bar off chest, flaring elbows too wide, lifting butt off bench

Incline Dumbbell Press

Setup:

  • Set bench to 30-45 degree incline
  • Hold dumbbells at shoulder level
  • Plant feet firmly on floor

Execution:

  1. Press dumbbells up and slightly together
  2. Lower dumbbells to chest level with control
  3. Feel stretch in chest at bottom
  4. Press up explosively
  5. Rotate dumbbells slightly inward at top

Common Mistakes: Using too steep an incline, pressing straight up instead of slight angle, dropping dumbbells too quickly

Dumbbell Flyes

Setup:

  • Lie on flat or incline bench
  • Hold dumbbells above chest with slight bend in elbows
  • Palms facing each other

Execution:

  1. Lower dumbbells in wide arc motion
  2. Feel stretch across chest
  3. Keep slight bend in elbows throughout
  4. Bring dumbbells together above chest
  5. Squeeze chest at top

Common Mistakes: Bending elbows too much (becomes a press), going too heavy, lowering dumbbells too far

BACK EXERCISES

Bent-Over Barbell Row

Setup:

  • Stand with feet hip-width apart
  • Hold barbell with overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulders
  • Hinge at hips, keeping back straight
  • Torso should be 45-degree angle or parallel to floor

Execution:

  1. Pull barbell to lower chest/upper abdomen
  2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
  3. Keep elbows close to body
  4. Lower bar with control
  5. Maintain rigid torso throughout

Common Mistakes: Rounding the back, using too much leg drive, pulling to wrong part of body

Weighted Pull-ups/Lat Pulldowns

Pull-ups Setup:

  • Hang from bar with overhand grip
  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders
  • Add weight with belt or hold dumbbell

Lat Pulldown Setup:

  • Sit at lat pulldown machine
  • Grab bar with wide overhand grip
  • Lean back slightly

Execution:

  1. Pull body up (pull-ups) or bar down (pulldowns)
  2. Lead with chest, not chin
  3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
  4. Lower with control
  5. Full stretch at bottom

Common Mistakes: Swinging or using momentum, pulling behind neck, not achieving full range of motion

SHOULDER EXERCISES

Overhead Press (Military Press)

Setup:

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
  • Hold barbell at shoulder level
  • Grip slightly wider than shoulders
  • Engage core

Execution:

  1. Press bar straight up over head
  2. Keep core tight throughout
  3. Don't arch back excessively
  4. Lock out arms at top
  5. Lower bar to starting position

Common Mistakes: Pressing bar forward instead of up, arching back too much, not engaging core

Lateral Raises

Setup:

  • Stand with feet hip-width apart
  • Hold dumbbells at sides
  • Slight bend in elbows
  • Engage core

Execution:

  1. Raise dumbbells out to sides
  2. Lead with pinkies, not thumbs
  3. Raise to shoulder height
  4. Hold briefly at top
  5. Lower with control

Common Mistakes: Using too much weight, swinging the weights, raising above shoulder level

LOWER BODY EXERCISES

QUAD-DOMINANT EXERCISES

Barbell Back Squat

Setup:

  • Position bar on upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar)
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
  • Toes slightly pointed out
  • Engage core

Execution:

  1. Initiate movement by pushing hips back
  2. Lower until thighs parallel to floor
  3. Keep knees tracking over toes
  4. Drive through heels to stand
  5. Maintain straight back throughout

Common Mistakes: Knees caving inward, rounding back, not going deep enough

HAMSTRING/GLUTE EXERCISES

Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

Setup:

  • Stand with feet hip-width apart
  • Hold barbell with overhand grip
  • Bar starts at hip level

Execution:

  1. Push hips back while lowering bar
  2. Keep bar close to legs
  3. Feel stretch in hamstrings
  4. Drive hips forward to return
  5. Maintain straight back

Common Mistakes: Bending knees too much, rounding back, lowering bar too far

Hip Thrusts

Setup:

  • Sit with upper back against bench
  • Place barbell across hips (use pad)
  • Feet flat on floor, shoulder-width apart

Execution:

  1. Drive through heels to lift hips
  2. Squeeze glutes at top
  3. Create straight line from knees to shoulders
  4. Lower hips with control
  5. Don't hyperextend back

Common Mistakes: Arching back too much, not squeezing glutes, using neck instead of upper back on bench

FULL BODY/CIRCUIT EXERCISES

COMPOUND MOVEMENTS

Burpees

Setup:

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
  • Arms at sides

Execution:

  1. Squat down and place hands on floor
  2. Jump feet back into plank position
  3. Perform push-up (optional)
  4. Jump feet back to squat position
  5. Jump up with arms overhead

Common Mistakes: Not maintaining plank position, skipping the push-up, not fully extending on jump

Kettlebell Swings

Setup:

  • Stand with feet slightly wider than shoulders
  • Hold kettlebell with both hands
  • Hinge at hips

Execution:

  1. Swing kettlebell between legs
  2. Drive hips forward explosively
  3. Swing kettlebell to chest height
  4. Let kettlebell swing back between legs
  5. Repeat with continuous motion

Common Mistakes: Using arms instead of hips, swinging too high, not hinging at hips

CORE EXERCISES

CORE STABILITY

Plank

Setup:

  • Start in push-up position
  • Lower to forearms
  • Keep body in straight line

Execution:

  1. Hold position without sagging
  2. Engage core throughout
  3. Keep head neutral
  4. Breathe normally
  5. Maintain straight line from head to heels

Common Mistakes: Sagging hips, raising hips too high, holding breath

Russian Twists

Setup:

  • Sit with knees bent, feet off floor
  • Hold weight with both hands
  • Lean back slightly

Execution:

  1. Rotate torso to one side
  2. Touch weight to floor beside hip
  3. Rotate to opposite side
  4. Keep feet off floor
  5. Maintain balance throughout

Common Mistakes: Moving too quickly, not rotating fully, putting feet down
